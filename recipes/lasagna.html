Lasagna

Recipe for a wonderful lasagne

Indulge in the rich layers of homemade lasagna, where tender pasta sheets embrace a savory blend of seasoned ground meat, creamy béchamel sauce, and a hearty tomato marinara. Topped with bubbling mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.

This classic Italian comfort dish is baked to perfection, offering a taste of pure comfort and satisfaction in every bite.

Ingredients

  • 9 Lasagna noodles
  • One pund ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 teaspoons Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for sautéing
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Noodles
    1. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions until they are al dente (firm to the bite).
    2. Drain the noodles and lay them flat on a clean surface to prevent sticking.
  2. Prepare the Meat Sauce
    1. In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat.
    2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, sautéing until they become fragrant and translucent.
    3. Add the ground beef and cook until it's browned and no longer pink, breaking it into small pieces with a spoon.
    4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Simmer the sauce for about 10 minutes, then remove it from heat.
  3. Prepare the Ricotta filling
    1. In a mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, 1 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese, 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese, and the eggs. Mix until well combined. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Assemble the Lasagna
    1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. In a 9x13-inch baking dish, spread a small amount of the meat sauce to prevent sticking.
    3. Place three lasagna noodles side by side over the sauce.
    4. Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture over the noodles.
    5. Add a layer of the meat sauce.
    6. Repeat the layers (noodles, ricotta, meat sauce) until you run out of ingredients, finishing with a layer of meat sauce on top.
  5. Bake
    1. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheese over the top layer of meat sauce.
    2. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil.
    3. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, removing the foil during the last 10 minutes to allow the cheese to brown and bubble.
  6. Serve
    1. Let the lasagna cool for a few minutes before slicing it into servings.
    2. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley if desired.
